The Town in 1747 – Die Stadt im Jahr 1747

By the mid-18th century, about 9,000 people were living in the ducal town on the Oker. Built as one of the first planned Renaissance towns in Germany, it presented itself as a seat of ducal power. Its architecture and princely building activities represented the Welf rule. The royal claim to the town is clearest in the layout of the streets. They lead to the imposing castle district, the “Dammfeste”. Impressive churches and grand market squares bear witness to unshakeable faith and a flourishing economy.
